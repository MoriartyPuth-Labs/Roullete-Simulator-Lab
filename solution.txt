Let's break it into a few parts.

Firstly, the easiest: the fields.
Fields, also less formally known as "global variables" keep their name, even after compiled, for compability with other java classes interacting with it reason.

One other thing that also keeps its original name are functions.

There are 3 functions in the code:

The secound function has less code to explain than the first, so I'll start there.

All it does is to apply the xorshift16 algorithm to the seed and return the same, after applying the algorithm.
Xorshift16 is a 16-bit RNG algorithm that cycles thru all 16 bit values, except 0, before looping.

Now that we fully understand the code, let's start working on the solution.

Our goal is to get negative money, but since we can't get it by losing more than the have, we have to reach the negatives by force by just getting so much money
that the sign bit turns into a 1. That's right, the solution is to get so much money that we overflow the ammount of money we have into the negatives.

Now you may be thinking "Well, so we just have to all in a couple times and get lucky", but that won't work.
Out of all 2^16 seeds, the only one that gets that many all ins in a row is seed 0, and the literal only reason why that happends is just because xorshift keeps
seed 0 at 0, and 0 % 37 happends to be less than 18.
But unless if you can time executing the program in exactly a multiple of 2^16 nano secound, you will not get the seed 0.


However, there is a propriety of the rng that we can abuse: once it chose its initial seed, all alterations to the seed are deterministic,
and additionally there only exist about 65 thousand possible seeds.

So here's how I solved this crackme:
	First, I execute the crackme and bet 1 unit about 20 times in a row.
	Then I input the pattern of wins and losses to another external program that will compare that pattern to all 65 thousand possible patterns in order to
		discover which seed I got.
	Then, using the seed, I generate the pattern of future wins and losses and, when I'm about to win go all in, and when I'm about to lose bet minimum (1 unit).

This way we can exponentially increase our money without risk and without guessing, eventually overflowing our money counter into the negatives and beating the crackme.
